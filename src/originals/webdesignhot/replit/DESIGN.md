## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

Replit's marketing site treats the IDE as the brand. The canvas is a near-black `#0e1525` ‚Äî the same colour the editor uses ‚Äî and almost every section anchors on a faux-editor screenshot with JetBrains Mono glyphs inside. The brand colour is a saturated turquoise `#1fe0ea` that does all the action work; secondary feature accents (orange, pink, purple) appear in context but never compete for the CTA. The page reads as *you're already inside the tool* ‚Äî there is no "marketing" layer separate from the product.

The atmosphere is electric. Where Vercel's dark mode reads as monastic black with a single white type wall, Replit pushes saturation: the cyan against navy ink is high-contrast enough to feel almost neon, and the secondary accents (warm orange for community, soft purple for AI, hot pink for Ghostwriter) introduce moments of chromatic energy that signal "this is a place where things happen." The `#0e1525` ink is intentionally a navy-tinted near-black ‚Äî slightly cooler than Linear's `#08090a`, slightly warmer than pure `#000000` ‚Äî and the cyan reads warmer against it than it would against true black.

The type system is "Replit Sans" ‚Äî a custom IBM Plex Sans derivative ‚Äî paired with JetBrains Mono. Headlines hit 72px in 600 weight with a tight `-0.025em` tracking, giving the marketing a confident bulletin quality. The slightly architectural Plex feel survives even at hero sizes. Body type is the same Replit Sans at 400/500, sized 17px on a 1.5 line-height. The mono is JetBrains Mono ‚Äî used for code blocks, terminal screenshots, and the small all-caps label moments at 12px with `0.04em` tracking. JetBrains Mono's unmistakable letterforms (zero with a dot, ligatures on `=>`, distinctive `g`) are part of Replit's brand fingerprint as much as the cyan is.

Depth is achieved through a surface ladder rather than shadow. The `#0b1120` ‚Üí `#0e1525` ‚Üí `#1c2333` ‚Üí `#2b3245` ladder lets cards lift via background change plus an 8‚Äì14% white hairline (`#ffffff14`, `#ffffff24`). Shadows, when they appear, are soft cool blacks beneath the faux-IDE screenshots, used to lift the "app" out of the page. Cards themselves are flat-on-flat. The cyan glow ring on the primary CTA hover is the single chromatic depth cue.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Iconic `#0e1525` navy-ink canvas ‚Äî not pure black, intentionally warmer
- Saturated turquoise `#1fe0ea` brand with `#0e1525` dark text on it ‚Äî never white text on cyan
- Custom Replit Sans (IBM Plex derivative) + JetBrains Mono ‚Äî the typographic backbone
- Faux-IDE hero ‚Äî editor chrome, tab strip, JetBrains Mono inside; anchors nearly every section
- Dense feature grids on `#1c2333` cards with `#ffffff14` hairline borders
- Surface-ladder depth instead of shadow ‚Äî flat-on-flat
- Secondary accents (orange / pink / purple) for feature contexts only ‚Äî never on the main CTA
- Tight `-0.025em` tracking on display headlines for bulletin quality
- 8px radius on buttons, 12px on cards ‚Äî IDE-rectangular, not SaaS-pillowy
- All-caps mono labels at 12px / `0.04em` tracking ‚Äî UI-tag aesthetic

## 3. Typography Rules

### Font Family

- **Primary sans**: Replit Sans ‚Äî a custom IBM Plex Sans derivative drawn for the brand.
- **Fallback chain**: `"Replit Sans", "IBM Plex Sans", -apple-system, "system-ui", "Segoe UI", Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif`.
- **Mono companion**: JetBrains Mono ‚Äî the editor typeface and brand fingerprint.
- **Mono fallback**: `"IBM Plex Mono", ui-monospace, SFMono-Regular, Menlo, Consolas, monospace`.
- **OpenType features** ‚Äî no `ss01` discipline like Stripe; standard ligatures and kerning. JetBrains Mono ligatures (`=>`, `!=`, `>=`) are intentionally enabled to read as "real code".

### Principles

- **Replit Sans is the body voice** ‚Äî both display and body use the same family. Plex-derived geometry survives at hero sizes.
- **Tracking tightens with size** ‚Äî `-0.03em` at 96px, `-0.025em` at 72px, `-0.02em` at 48px, normal below 18px. Display feels confident, body feels neutral.
- **JetBrains Mono is brand fingerprint** ‚Äî it appears wherever code, label, terminal, or all-caps tag appears. Its ligatures are intentionally on.
- **No serif anywhere** ‚Äî Replit's identity is sans display + mono accent. A serif would break the IDE metaphor.
- **Two weight families** ‚Äî sans uses 400 (body), 500 (nav/label), 600 (display/button). Mono uses 400 (code), 500 (label), 600 (bold code).
- **Weight 600 is the headline weight** ‚Äî Replit goes confident-bold rather than Stripe-light. The brand is *builder*, not *editorialist*.
- **Mono labels at 0.04em tracking** ‚Äî UI-tag aesthetic borrowed from CLI prompts. All caps for "NEW" / "BETA" / "AI" goes to `0.08em`.

## 5. Layout Principles

### Spacing System

- Base unit: `8px`
- Scale: `2, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64, 80, 96, 120, 160`
- Density observation: dense by marketing standards. Replit packs more information per scroll than Vercel or Linear, in keeping with the "this is a tool for builders" positioning. The `8px` rhythm holds throughout.

### Grid & Container

- Max content width: `1280px` with `24px` gutters
- Hero bands often have a faux-IDE screenshot taking 60% of the horizontal space
- Feature grids are typically three-column with `#1c2333` cards
- Code blocks may break out to wider widths, sometimes full-bleed
- Vertical rhythm runs `96‚Äì120px` between sections

### Whitespace Philosophy

- **Dense over airy** ‚Äî Replit prioritises information density. Three-column feature grids and stacked code blocks are the norm.
- **Editor-pane proportions** ‚Äî section widths and card aspect ratios echo IDE pane geometry rather than golden-ratio editorial.
- **Breathing room around code** ‚Äî code blocks always get extra padding (`24‚Äì32px`) to feel like they "belong" rather than cramming.

### Section Cadence

- Hero (faux-IDE on `#0e1525`) ‚Üí feature grid (3-column on `#0e1525`) ‚Üí terminal demo band (often `#0b1120` for contrast) ‚Üí testimonial/logo wall ‚Üí pricing ‚Üí CTA ‚Üí footer
- No light-section breaks ‚Äî the canvas stays dark throughout marketing
- Surface-ladder swaps (`#0e1525` ‚Üî `#0b1120`) provide rhythm without breaking the dark-mode discipline

## 6. Shapes & Radius Scale

The radii ladder is `4/6/8/12/16`. Buttons land at `8px`, cards at `12px`, and the faux-IDE chrome at `12px` to mimic a macOS window. There are no fully-rounded CTAs ‚Äî the shape vocabulary stays rectangular, like an editor. Pills (`9999`) appear on tags and badges only.

## 7. Depth & Elevation

| Level | Treatment | Use |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| 0 ‚Äî Flat | No shadow, surface ladder | Page canvas, body cards |
| 1 ‚Äî Hairline | `1px solid #ffffff14` | Default card and panel separation |
| 2 ‚Äî Surface Lift | Surface bg ‚Üí next ladder rung + `1px solid #ffffff24` | Hover and elevated panels |
| 3 ‚Äî Soft Shadow | `0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.25)` plus hairline | Faux-IDE screenshots lifted off canvas |
| 4 ‚Äî Elevated | `0 16px 40px rgba(0,0,0,0.4), 0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.25)` | Hero IDE frames, modals |
| 5 ‚Äî Cyan Glow | `0 0 24px rgba(31,224,234,0.3)` | Primary CTA hover state |
| 6 ‚Äî Ring | `2px solid #1fe0ea` at `2px` offset | Keyboard focus |

**Shadow Philosophy** ‚Äî depth is achieved through the surface ladder (`#0b1120` ‚Üí `#0e1525` ‚Üí `#1c2333` ‚Üí `#2b3245`) and 8‚Äì14% white hairlines (`#ffffff14`, `#ffffff24`). Shadows are minimal ‚Äî when they appear, they're soft cool blacks beneath the faux-IDE screenshots, used to lift the "app" out of the page. Cards themselves are flat-on-flat. The cyan glow ring is the single chromatic depth cue and appears only on the primary CTA hover.

## 8. Interaction & Motion

### Easing Curves

- **Standard** `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` ‚Äî default
- **Emphasized** `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)` ‚Äî hero entrance, glow ring expansion
- **Snappy** `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` ‚Äî quick, slight overshoot for IDE-like state changes

### Duration Buckets

- Fast: `120ms` ‚Äî colour swaps, ring fades
- Standard: `200ms` ‚Äî button hover, card lift, dropdown open
- Slow: `320ms` ‚Äî hero reveals, faux-IDE typewriter animation

### Per-Component Micro-States

- **Button hover (primary)** ‚Äî background `#1fe0ea` ‚Üí `#3deef7` over 120ms; cyan glow ring expands to `0 0 24px rgba(31,224,234,0.3)` over 200ms.
- **Card hover** ‚Äî background ‚Üí next surface-ladder rung, border ‚Üí `#ffffff24`, optional `translate-Y(-2px)` over 200ms standard ease.
- **Link hover** ‚Äî colour `#f5f9fc` ‚Üí `#1fe0ea` over 120ms; underline does NOT appear (links are colour-shift only on dark canvas).
- **Input focus** ‚Äî border `#ffffff24` ‚Üí `#1fe0ea` plus 2px cyan ring `rgba(31,224,234,0.3)` over 120ms.
- **Editor frame entrance** ‚Äî opacity 0‚Üí1 plus `translate-Y(16px)` over 320ms emphasized, triggered by Intersection Observer.
- **Faux typewriter** ‚Äî code inside the editor frame types out at ~60ms per character, looped, with cursor blink at 600ms cycle.

### Page Transitions

- No client-side transitions on marketing. Standard browser navigation. Hero modules animate in over 320ms once in viewport.

### Reduced Motion Strategy

- `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` ‚Äî opacity-only fades at 120ms linear. Translate, glow pulse, and typewriter animation are removed entirely. Static code shown instead of animated typing.

## 9. Accessibility & A11y

- `#1fe0ea` link on `#0e1525` bg ‚Äî **12.1:1** (AAA)

- `#9b6bff` accent on `#0e1525` bg ‚Äî **5.2:1** (AA Large)

### Focus Indicators

- Form inputs use a 2px cyan ring inset

### ARIA Patterns

- **Combobox** ‚Äî `role="combobox"` with `aria-expanded`, `aria-controls` to listbox id (used in IDE-pattern command palette references)

- **Tablist** ‚Äî `role="tablist"` for the faux-IDE tab strip

### Keyboard Navigation

- Tab order follows DOM source order
- Skip-to-content link in header (visible on focus only)
- All interactive elements reachable via Tab
- `Esc` closes dropdowns, modals, command palettes
- Arrow keys navigate IDE-pattern lists (file tree, tablist, menubar)
- `Cmd+K` / `Ctrl+K` opens command palette where present

### Screen Reader Hints

- Decorative cyan dots and glow rings `aria-hidden="true"`
- Faux-IDE screenshots labelled with `aria-label="Code example showing [feature]"` plus visible caption
- "NEW" / "BETA" / "AI" badges read aloud as "New feature", "Beta feature", "AI feature"

### Reduced Motion

## 13. Lineage & Influences

Replit's marketing aesthetic is what happens when an IDE escapes onto a landing page. The canvas is the iconic `#0e1525` near-black ink ‚Äî slightly cooler than Linear's `#08090a`, slightly warmer than Vercel's pure black ‚Äî and the brand colour is a saturated turquoise `#1fe0ea` that lights up the whole page from the CTA.

The type system is house-customised: "Replit Sans" is a Plex-Sans derivative drawn for the brand, with JetBrains Mono as the inevitable companion for code samples and label-as-mono moments. Secondary accents ‚Äî `#ff8a3d` orange (community / Bounty), `#f26d9f` pink, and `#9b6bff` purple (AI / Ghostwriter) ‚Äî appear in feature-specific contexts but never on the main CTA, which is always cyan.

The defining visual move is the **faux-IDE hero**: nearly every section anchors on a screenshot of an editor pane, terminal, or run output, with JetBrains Mono syntax-highlighted inside. The lineage runs through Vercel's dark-mode marketing discipline, Linear's flat-on-flat depth, and the cloud-IDE visual heritage that started with Cloud9 and StackBlitz ‚Äî but Replit pushes harder on the *I write code in the browser* identity than any of them.

The brand explicitly rejects: gradient backgrounds, illustrated mascots, soft pastel palettes, pill-shaped CTAs, serif type anywhere, white-on-cyan buttons (the dark-on-cyan rule is doctrinal), pure-black `#000000` canvases (Replit's `#0e1525` is intentionally a navy-ink), and any visual move that would read as "consumer SaaS" rather than "developer tool."

**Named influences:**
- IBM Plex / JetBrains Mono ‚Äî the two foundries that supply Replit's typographic identity
- Vercel ‚Äî dark-mode marketing discipline, single saturated brand accent
- Cloud9 / StackBlitz ‚Äî cloud-IDE visual heritage
- Linear ‚Äî flat-on-flat depth via surface ladder

## 14. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Put dark `#0e1525` text on the cyan CTA ‚Äî never white. The contrast is what makes the turquoise read as electric.
- Use JetBrains Mono everywhere code or label appears ‚Äî it's a brand fingerprint, not a placeholder.
- Anchor sections on faux-IDE screenshots ‚Äî the "you're looking at a real editor" illusion is core.
- Use the surface ladder (`#0b1120` / `#0e1525` / `#1c2333` / `#2b3245`) for depth instead of shadow.
- Apply the cyan glow ring only to the primary CTA hover ‚Äî never to other interactive elements.
- Keep button radius at 8px and card radius at 12px ‚Äî IDE-rectangular, not SaaS-pillowy.
- Use mono labels at 12px / `0.04em` tracking for status tags and section eyebrows.
- Enable JetBrains Mono ligatures ‚Äî `=>`, `!=`, `>=` ligatures are intentional brand cues.
- Cap reading width around 720px on body text despite the dense layout ‚Äî readability remains primary.
- Use weight 600 for display headlines ‚Äî the bulletin-confident voice is the brand.

### Don't

- Don't mix the secondary accents (orange / pink / purple) into the main CTA. Cyan owns the action layer.
- Don't lift cards with shadows; depth is the surface ladder plus a hairline.
- Don't drift into pure black `#000000` ‚Äî Replit's `#0e1525` is intentionally a navy-ink.
- Don't use white text on the cyan CTA ‚Äî the dark-text-on-cyan contrast is doctrinal.
- Don't introduce a serif typeface anywhere ‚Äî sans + mono is the entire system.
- Don't pill-shape CTAs ‚Äî the shape vocabulary is rectangular like an editor.
- Don't apply gradients to backgrounds ‚Äî flat ink is the canvas.
- Don't soften display tracking ‚Äî `-0.025em` at 72px is the bulletin signature.
- Don't use the cyan glow on every hover ‚Äî its scarcity is what makes it land.
- Don't strip JetBrains Mono ligatures ‚Äî disabling them removes a brand cue.

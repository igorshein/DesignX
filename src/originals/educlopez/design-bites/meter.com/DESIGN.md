# meter.com — Design System Reference

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

Serious infrastructure with a purple soul. Meter.com chooses a cool-blue-purple slate palette — steel grays tinting toward blue-violet — that signals enterprise networking infrastructure without the cold sterility of typical DevOps palettes. The brand cobalt (rgb(84,97,200)) acts as both primary action and product identity. Mint green and periwinkle semantic chips add accessible status clarity without breaking the cool palette logic. Suisse Intl (a neo-grotesque) lends Swiss precision to every character.

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Use the exact colour roles as defined — each serves a specific semantic purpose
- Respect the typeface role boundaries (each font has one job)
- Preserve generous whitespace — crowding breaks the design language
- Keep interactions restrained: hover opacity or colour-shift, not complex transforms

**Don't:**
- Introduce colours outside the defined palette
- Mix typefaces beyond the established pairing
- Add drop shadows where the design is intentionally flat
- Use gradients unless the site already employs them
- Add Warm tones
- Add decorative gradients
